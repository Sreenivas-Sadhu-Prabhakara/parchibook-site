The problem

A busy shop runs on WhatsApp, a diary, and memory. All three leak.

01

Orders scatter across chats

Thirty messages come in during the evening rush. You scroll, copy each onto a paper parchi, and one order quietly gets missed.

02

Pricing lives in your head

Rates change with the mandi. You total five items from memory at the counter, undercharge a little on each, and never notice the slow bleed.

03

The khata falls behind

“Likh dena” means it should go in the diary. By closing time you can’t remember who took what on udhaar, so the balance is a guess.

04

Delivery is a scramble

The delivery boy leaves with a fistful of parchis in no order, doubles back across the same gully twice, and forgets one house.

The insight

The order is already typed. The shopkeeper is re-doing, by hand, work the customer already finished.

So don’t build another ordering app nobody will download. Read the WhatsApp message that’s already there, match each line to your saved rates, and let the parchi, the bill, and the khata write themselves.

How it works

Paste the chat. Get a bill. Done in four steps.

  1. 1

    Paste the order

    Copy the customer’s WhatsApp message and paste it in. ParchiBook reads it line by line — “2 kg aata”, “Maggi 4 packet”.

  2. 2

    It matches your catalog

    Each line is matched to your saved items by name or alias — case doesn’t matter, spelling need not be exact — and priced from your rate.

  3. 3

    You fix any misses

    Anything it can’t match is flagged. Tap once to add the item and rate; next time it’s recognised on its own.

  4. 4

    Bill, list & khata

    Out comes an itemized bill for the customer, a delivery-run list for the boy, and the amount is posted to that customer’s khata.

What it does

Everything the parchi used to do, minus the pen.

Line-by-line parsing

Reads a pasted WhatsApp order one line at a time, pulling out quantity, unit and item name.

Smart catalog match

Matches each line to your items by name or alias — case-insensitive and forgiving of typos — then prices it from your saved rate.

Unmatched-line flags

Anything unfamiliar is set aside with a one-tap “add to catalog”, so no line is silently dropped.

Itemized bill

A clean bill with rates, quantities and total — ready to send back on WhatsApp or print for delivery.

Delivery-run list

Every order for the round rolls up into one picking-and-delivery list, so the boy packs once and doesn’t double back.

Auto-posted khata

The bill total drops straight onto the customer’s running balance — no separate diary entry, no end-of-day guessing.

Do my customers need to install anything?

No. They keep ordering on WhatsApp exactly as they do now. ParchiBook only sits on your side, turning their message into a bill.

What if it can’t read an item?

That line is flagged, not dropped. You tap once to add the item and its rate; from then on it’s matched automatically — the more you use it, the smarter your catalog gets.

Does it work in Hindi and Hinglish?

Yes. It reads the way your customers actually type — “2 kg aata”, “doodh 1”, “chai patti 250g” — and matches on names and aliases you set in your own words.

How much does ParchiBook cost?

Pricing is worked out on a quick discovery call and billed monthly on subscription. We’re keeping public prices off the page while we onboard our first users.
